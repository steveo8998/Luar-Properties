Employer Profiles

The Big Three Employment Engines

Blog content should directly reference these employers by name — that's exactly what their workers are searching when they get their assignment or job offer.

OSF HealthCare / Saint Francis Medical Center

Greater Peoria's largest employer. OSF HealthCare operates 17 hospitals across Illinois and Michigan, but the flagship is right here in Peoria. It's a massive teaching hospital affiliated with the University of Illinois College of Medicine.

Employees 13,500+ in Greater Peoria (26,000 system-wide)
Facility 600+ bed Level I Trauma Center, Children's Hospital of Illinois, Cardiovascular Institute
Housing demand Travel nurses (13-week contracts, often extended), medical residents, clinical students, locum tenens physicians, traveling surgical techs
Also nearby UnityPoint Health Methodist, Saint Francis Medical Center College of Nursing

Caterpillar Inc.

Peoria is still CAT's operational heart — the largest manufacturing campus and ~90% of the workforce remain here even after the corporate HQ moved to Deerfield. This is a Fortune 500 company with a constant stream of project-based contractors, engineers, and interns.

Workforce Tens of thousands (direct + contractors). Region's 2nd largest employer cluster.
Facility Manufacturing, R&D, Caterpillar Visitor Center
Housing demand Project-based contractors (3–12 months), summer interns (May–Aug), consultants, relocating employees, visiting engineers
Key detail CAT often provides relocation allowances or contractor housing stipends

Rivian Automotive

The EV manufacturer's only active production plant is in Normal, IL — 38 miles east of Peoria. 2026 is a massive year: the R2 SUV line is launching, a new 1.2M sq ft supplier park is being completed, and production is ramping toward 215,000 vehicles/year.

Employees 8,000+ at Normal plant, plus hundreds more coming with supplier park
Investment $1.5B in R2 expansion + $120M supplier park (completing 2026)
Drive from Peoria 38 miles, ~40 minutes via I-74 and US-150
Housing demand Production workers, automation technicians, tool & die, paint techs, engineers, construction workers building the supplier park, EV supplier employees relocating
Key detail Bloomington-Normal housing is tighter and more expensive. Peoria offers a cheaper alternative with an easy commute. Shift workers on 2nd/3rd shift commute against traffic.

Timing matters: Rivian's R2 production launch and supplier park completion in 2026 will create a surge in temporary housing demand in the region. Blog content targeting "Rivian housing" keywords should be published now to establish rankings before the wave hits.

Competitive Landscape

Where Mid-Term Renters Currently Search

Nate's blog content needs to outperform these platforms in search for employer-specific and relocation queries — which is achievable because none of them publish local content.

Platform Model Weakness
Furnished Finder $199/yr listing fee, no commission. 151 listings in Peoria. The dominant platform for travel nurse housing. No local content. Listings look identical. No SEO for employer-specific queries. Nate's listing competes with 150 others on the same page.
Airbnb (Monthly) 14-16% guest service fee + 3% host fee. Monthly discount option available but guests still pay platform fees. Expensive for 30+ day stays due to fees. No employer-targeted content. Algorithm-dependent visibility.
Apartments.com 188 short-term listings in Peoria. $862–$1,251/mo average. Mostly apartment complexes. Impersonal. No furnished homes. Not designed for the "I need a home, not a unit" renter.
Extended Stay America Hotel chain with kitchenettes. Weekly/monthly rates. It's a hotel room. No privacy, no yard, no washer/dryer, no home feeling. Fine for 1-2 weeks, terrible for 3+ months.
CorporateHousing.com 191 listings in Peoria area. Broker-style — they match tenants with properties. Intermediary adds cost. No direct relationship. Generic listings.

Nate's edge: A direct booking site with blog content that speaks to specific employers and situations. "I'm a travel nurse starting at OSF in 3 weeks" or "I just took a job at Rivian and need a place to live" — Nate's blog post answers that exact query, and the CTA takes them straight to a booking with no platform fees, no competing listings, and a real person (Nate) on the other end.

Content Strategy

How the Blog Drives Bookings

The Funnel

Every blog post serves the same conversion path:

  • Search: Professional gets job offer / travel assignment → Googles "travel nurse housing Peoria IL" or "relocating to Peoria for Caterpillar"
  • Discover: Nate's blog post is the top organic result because no platform creates this content
  • Trust: The post reads like a local who knows the area, not a generic listing platform
  • Convert: CTA at the bottom → "Book direct at Luar Properties — furnished, flexible, no platform fees. [Property Name] is 10 minutes from OSF / 40 minutes from Rivian."

Blog Post Template for This Audience

  • H1 with employer name + housing keyword — These people search by employer name
  • Practical tone, not salesy — Write like you're giving advice to a coworker, not pitching a product
  • Include drive times, grocery stores, gyms, pharmacies — This is what a relocator actually needs
  • Property tie-in feels natural, not forced — "We offer furnished rentals starting at 30 days — here's what's available"
  • Add a neighborhood map or commute map — Visual content gets shared and linked to

Publishing Priority

  • Month 1: Publish all 5 Tier 1 employer pages + the neighborhood guide
  • Month 2: Publish remaining Tier 2 lifestyle posts
  • Month 3+: One Tier 3 seasonal post per month, plus update Tier 1 posts with new info

Distribution Beyond Google

These blog posts shouldn't just sit on the site waiting for Google to rank them. They should be actively shared in:

  • Travel nurse Facebook groups — "Travel Nurses - Peoria IL," "Travel Nurse Housing Tips," etc.
  • Rivian worker forums and Facebook groups — Rivian employees actively discuss housing options online
  • Reddit — r/TravelNursing, r/Rivian, r/PeoriaIL
  • Furnished Finder profile — Link to the blog from Nate's Furnished Finder listing to drive traffic to the direct site
  • Hospital bulletin boards — OSF and UnityPoint have physical and digital boards for travel staff resources

Bottom line: The blog isn't a "nice to have" for mid-term rentals — it's the entire acquisition strategy. Nate's properties aren't competing with 10,000 Airbnb listings on a search page. They're competing with a handful of corporate housing brokers and platform listings that all look the same. One well-written blog post that says "I'm a local host in Peoria, here's what you need to know about working at OSF / CAT / Rivian" will outperform all of them in organic search.
